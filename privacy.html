How we handle your information

Effective date: May 26, 2026 · Last updated: May 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how PebblePath ("PebblePath," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use the PebblePath mobile application and the website at pebblepath.ai (together, the "Service").

PebblePath is a tool for parents and caregivers to track their child's developmental milestones, receive personalized guidance, and prepare for school transitions. The Service is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children directly. See Children's Information below for detail.

If you have any questions about this Policy, contact us at thomas@pebblepath.ai.

1. Who is the data controller

PebblePath is operated by Thomas Paris (the "data controller"). Contact: thomas@pebblepath.ai.

If we form a legal entity (e.g., PebblePath LLC), this section will be updated and the Effective date above bumped.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide directly

  • Account information — name, email address, password (hashed by our authentication provider), and optionally a profile photo. If you sign in with Google or (in future) Apple, we receive your name, email, and a unique identifier from that provider.
  • Family information — a family display name and an invite code we generate so you can add a co-parent.
  • Child information you enter about your child — name, date of birth, region (e.g., state for school timeline localization), photo, developmental flags (e.g., "speech / language"), and any pediatrician or personal notes you choose to add.
  • Milestone records — which milestones you've marked as not started, emerging, or achieved, plus any notes or photos you attach.
  • Tip interactions — which tips you've read or marked actionable.
  • Conversations with the AI advisor ("Pebble") — the questions you send, the model's responses, and metadata such as the time and the child context that accompanied the question.
  • Payment information (when subscriptions launch) — Apple processes all in-app purchases. We never see your credit card number or full billing details. Apple shares with us only a subscription status, plan, and an anonymized transaction identifier.
  • Support communications — anything you send us by email.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Device and usage data — device model, operating system version, app version, language and region settings, crash logs, and basic interaction events (which screens you opened, button taps).
  • Identifiers — Firebase installation ID and Apple's IDFV (Identifier for Vendor). We do not use IDFA for advertising.
  • Diagnostic data — performance traces and stability data via Firebase Crashlytics (when enabled).

2.3 Information from third parties

  • Authentication providers (Google; Apple, when added) provide name, email, and a unique identifier when you sign in with them.
  • Apple App Store (when subscriptions launch) provides subscription status and renewal events.

We do not purchase or receive personal information from data brokers or advertising networks.

4. The AI advisor ("Pebble") — third-party processing

When you ask Pebble a question or generate a batch of tips, we send the relevant child context (name, age, focus areas, achieved/emerging milestones) and your question to Anthropic, PBC ("Anthropic"), the maker of the Claude AI models we use. Anthropic processes the request and returns a response, which we display to you and store on our servers.

  • Anthropic does not train its models on data sent through the API. (See Anthropic's Privacy Policy and Commercial Terms.)
  • Anthropic retains API request data for a limited operational period (typically 30 days for safety review) before deletion.
  • We do not send your name, email, or contact information to Anthropic — only the child profile context needed to produce a useful answer.

4.1 Web search

For questions that benefit from current, public information (e.g. "what kid-friendly museums are open this Saturday near us?"), Pebble may use Anthropic's hosted web search tool. The search runs on Anthropic's infrastructure; we do not operate or read the underlying searches. Only the question text and the relevant child context (which never includes your name, email, or contact details) are passed to the tool. Search results are returned to Pebble, which uses them to compose its answer; the raw results are not separately stored on our servers.

4.2 Synthesized continuity profile

To help Pebble provide useful guidance over time (rather than starting fresh each conversation), our backend periodically generates a short synthesized continuity profile for each child you've added. This profile is created from data you've already provided in PebblePath — recent conversations with Pebble, milestones marked as achieved, observations you've logged, and which tips or insights you've engaged with — and is sent back to Pebble at the start of future conversations as context.

  • The synthesis is generated on a schedule (typically nightly) by Anthropic's Claude model on our backend. The same Anthropic data-handling terms in this section apply.
  • The profile is used only to personalize your own Pebble responses for your own child. It is not used to train any model, and it is not shared with anyone outside your family group (your co-parent who has joined the same family is, by design, the same audience).
  • You can delete the underlying conversation history at any time from the Pebble screen, which clears the input that the next synthesis would draw on.

We may add or substitute AI providers in the future. If we do, we will update this section before doing so.

6. Data retention

  • Active account data — kept for as long as your account is active.
  • Deleted account — when you delete your account, we delete or anonymize your data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law (e.g., billing records for tax purposes).
  • Pebble conversation history — stored on our servers indefinitely while your account is active so you can review past advice; you can clear it at any time from the Pebble screen.
  • Backup snapshots — encrypted backups may persist for up to 90 days after primary deletion.
  • Apple subscription receipts — retained for the period required by tax and consumer-protection laws (typically 7 years in the US).

To exercise any of these rights, email thomas@pebblepath.ai. We will respond within 30 days (45 days where law permits an extension).

8. Children's information

PebblePath is for parents and caregivers. We do not direct the Service to children under 13 and we do not knowingly accept accounts from anyone under 18.

You — the parent or caregiver — choose to enter information about your child. You are the data subject for your account; your child is not a user of the Service. You provide and control the child information.

  • We do not display advertising to anyone, child or adult.
  • The app has no public profiles, comments, social feeds, or messaging features that involve a child directly.
  • We collect from your child only what you choose to enter: a name, date of birth, region, optional photo, optional flags, and any notes you write.
  • You can delete your child's profile at any time from Settings, which removes all associated milestone, tip, and event data.
  • If you believe a child has somehow set up their own account on PebblePath, contact us at thomas@pebblepath.ai and we will delete the account.

This approach is consistent with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) framework for parent-facing services. We are not a "child-directed" service under 16 CFR Part 312.

9. Security

We use commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • TLS/HTTPS for data in transit between the app and our servers.
  • Encryption at rest for data stored in Cloud Firestore, Cloud Storage, and authentication systems.
  • AI advisor credentials are held server-side: requests to the AI advisor (Pebble) route through hardened cloud functions that verify your identity before processing. AI provider credentials are never embedded in the mobile app or transmitted from your device.
  • Personal name minimization in AI processing: before any request reaches our AI provider (Anthropic), our server replaces children's first names with non-identifying placeholders (e.g., {{CHILD_NAME}}). The placeholder is replaced with the actual name on our server before the response is returned to your device, so the AI provider's servers never receive the actual name. Other inputs (date of birth, milestone titles, mood summaries, parent observation text) still transit because they are the meaningful context required for personalized guidance.
  • Hardware-attested request verification via Firebase App Check: every request to our database, file storage, and cloud functions must carry an unforgeable token proving it originates from the genuine PebblePath app on a real device (Apple App Attest on iOS) or a real browser session (reCAPTCHA Enterprise on the web).
  • Server-enforced privacy for messages marked private: when you mark a Pebble conversation as private, the database itself refuses to return those messages to anyone other than the sender (enforced via per-message visibility lists in our database security rules — not a client-side filter that could be bypassed).
  • Optional biometric lock on the iOS app: a setting (off by default) requires Face ID, Touch ID, or device passcode each time you open PebblePath after being away for more than 30 seconds. Biometric data never leaves your device; authentication happens entirely in the iPhone's secure enclave.
  • Tamper-resistant audit log: sensitive administrative actions (removing a child profile, regenerating an invite code, granting another user access to a child) write tamper-resistant entries to our database. Only our Cloud Functions (running with administrative privileges) can write these entries; clients cannot edit or delete them. Family members can view this log via the app's Settings → Security → Admin Activity log surface.
  • Web security headers on pebblepath.ai: our web property serves a Content Security Policy, Strict-Transport-Security (HSTS), X-Frame-Options, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy, and Permissions-Policy on every response, protecting against script injection, clickjacking, MIME-type confusion, downgrade attacks, and unwanted referrer leakage.
  • Role-based access in our backend; no PebblePath employee or contractor has routine access to child profile data without an operational reason.
  • Strict per-child access rules: only a child's parents can read or modify that child's developmental data by default. Other family or extended-circle members must be explicitly granted access by a parent.
  • Multi-factor authentication on all administrative accounts.
  • Per-platform device protection: photos picked from your library remain on your device unless you choose to use the co-parent photo handoff feature, which uploads briefly to encrypted Cloud Storage and is deleted after the co-parent's device receives it.

No system is perfectly secure. If we detect a breach affecting your information, we will notify you and, where required, the relevant authorities, within the timelines required by law.
